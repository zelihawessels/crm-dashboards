Executive Summary

Jan 2025 – Apr 2026 · Promo email · 16 months

Thrive Market's promo email program sends daily blasts to active members. This analysis examines 16 months of performance (Jan 2025–Apr 2026) across 14 days-since-last-order segments to assess whether send volume, cadence, and suppression thresholds are calibrated to both revenue return and list health.

Volume and list health The 121+ days and No Orders segments account for ~49% of promo sends and return $0.004 and $0.002 RPD respectively — the lowest of any segment. This reflects list composition, not send frequency. The same two segments generate ~3,000 optouts/month (~36,200/year), representing 58% of all program optouts: No Orders at 0.021% (~1,400/month), 121+ at 0.015% (~1,600/month).
Best-performing window The 8–14 day post-purchase window averages $0.67 Rev/Delivered — roughly 20× the 121+ segment. April 2026 reached $0.70, the highest monthly figure on record. Within this window, Power Members (8+ orders) return $0.82 RPD vs $0.33 for Onboarding members, and non-autoship members return 4.5× more attributed RPD than autoship-active members. The overall program average of $0.67 masks this spread.
Audience tier performance Across the full program, Power Members (8+ orders) return $0.154 RPD per send — 13× more than Onboarding members ($0.012) and 4× more than Habit Formation ($0.041). Power Members and Onboarding each average ~13–14M sends per month, so the volume exposure is roughly equivalent while the return gap is large. Onboarding's optout rate of 0.021% matches the No Orders segment — the highest in the program.
Autoship status and email response Within the 8–14 day window, NOT Active members are the most responsive to promos ($1.51 RPD) and Active (w/ items) the least ($0.34). But overall program RPD tells a different story: Active (no items) leads ($0.086), Active (w/ items) middle ($0.076), NOT Active lowest ($0.067). The reason: NOT Active members purchase more sporadically — their sends are concentrated in 121+ and No Orders where per-send returns approach zero. Excluding No Orders, NOT Active recovers to $0.090, above Active (w/ items) at $0.076, which shows that among ordering members specifically, NOT Active are more valuable per send. Active (no items) members purchase more consistently, producing the highest RPD at both the overall and ordering-only levels. NOT Active members return $1.51 RPD at 8-14 days — the highest of any autoship group in the active window — but only $0.004 and $0.001 RPD once they lapse into 121+ and No Orders. At 7.8M/month in 121+ and 5.3M/month in No Orders at near-zero return, their lapsed sends account for a disproportionate share of program volume, making suppression of this group the highest-ROI action.
Priority actions
1 Run a holdout on 121+ and No Orders (primary cells), plus 15–30 days as a reference baseline — stratified by autoship status. This establishes which attributed revenue is incremental before any suppression decisions are made. Full detail in Recommended Next Steps.
2 Route No Orders members (no click in 180+ days) to a re-engagement flow and remove from daily sends. Projected: ~1,400 fewer optouts/month.
3 Reduce 121+ cadence from daily to 3×/week for non-autoship, non-Power Members. Projected: ~800–1,000 fewer optouts/month. Size revenue impact against holdout data first.

Key Findings

Jan 2025 – Apr 2026

The findings below cover the program's return per send and list health by segment, and identify where current cadence and suppression thresholds are misaligned.

8–14 Days: Highest Return Segment
~9.6% of promo sends. CTR 1.72%, Purchase CVR 0.754%, RPD $0.67 — the highest of any segment by a wide margin. By audience tier: Power Members (8+ orders) account for ~67% of 8-14 sends and return $0.82 RPD vs $0.33 for Onboarding — a 2.5× gap within the same window. By autoship status: NOT Active members return $1.51 RPD vs $0.34 for Active (w/ items), indicating members without recurring orders are more likely to make incremental purchases when prompted.
121+ Days: Volume Without Returns
At ~30% of promo sends and growing, the 121+ segment returns $0.004 RPD — less than 1% of the 8–14 day rate — with a 0.37% CTR. It generates ~1,600 optouts/month (~19,100/year). By autoship status: NOT Active members account for ~70% of 121+ sends and represent the clearest suppression case. Power Members in 121+ still return $0.008 RPD — double the segment average — which warrants separate holdout treatment before suppression is applied to this bucket.
No Orders: Lowest Return, Highest Optout
~19% of promo sends. RPD $0.002 and Purchase CVR 0.002% — the lowest in the program. Optout rate 0.021%, nearly 2× the program average, generating ~1,400 optouts/month (~17,000/year). There is no high-value sub-group within No Orders to protect — suppression applies uniformly here.
Productive Window: 4–30 Days
CTR 0.84–1.72%, CVR 0.03–0.75%, RPD $0.023–$0.67, optout rates at program lows of 0.009–0.013%. Current suppression logic is functioning as intended. Power Members and NOT Active members drive the majority of attributed revenue across this range. Active (no items) members outperform Active (w/ items) at every recency level within the window, and Active (w/ items) shows the lowest responsiveness even here — consistent with their pattern across the full program.
AOV Peaks at 15–30 Days
The 15–30 day segment has the highest AOV at ~$102; the 4–14 day window averages ~$89–90; No Orders lowest at ~$84. By audience tier, Power Members drive the premium at $104.5 in this window vs $97.7 for Habit Formation. By autoship status, Active (no items) leads at $104.8, closely followed by Active (w/ items) at $103.7. Suppressing lapsed segments reduces optouts and lifts program average order quality.
April 2026: Current Trends Stable
April 2026 shows consistent patterns with no material deterioration. 8-14 day segment at CTR 1.80%, PCVR 0.77%, RPD $0.70 — all above 16-month averages. 121+ and No Orders remain elevated in volume share (~49% combined), reinforcing the suppression opportunity.

Audience & Autoship Cuts

Segmented by audience tier and autoship status

The segment data above shows a volume-return mismatch at the program level. The two dimensions below explain who is driving that imbalance. Audience tier and autoship status produce substantially different return profiles within the same recency bucket. Cards are grouped by dimension — audience tier first, then autoship status — to show where the performance differences originate and which sub-groups warrant separate treatment in any suppression design.

Power Members drive 8-14 day performance
Power Members (8+ orders) return $0.82 RPD in the 8–14 day window — 23% above the $0.67 program average — and account for ~66% of 8–14 day volume. Their 121+ segment returns $0.008 RPD, double the program average for that bucket. Suppression logic for Power Members should be evaluated independently.
Habit Formation: stable mid-tier
Habit Formation members (3–7 orders) return $0.41 RPD at 8–14 days and $0.041 overall across all segments — above Onboarding ($0.012) but below Power Members ($0.154). 42% of HF sends fall in the 121+ bucket — the highest lapse concentration of any audience tier. These are members who completed 3–7 purchases at some point but have since disengaged; establishing an early purchase history does not guarantee sustained engagement. Their 121+ RPD of $0.003 is near the segment floor, meaning lapsed HF members return similarly to lapsed Onboarding members.
Onboarding tier: largest send volume, lowest return
Onboarding members (0–2 orders) return $0.33 RPD in the 8–14 day window vs $0.67 overall. Most Onboarding volume is in No Orders (6.9M/month avg). No Orders optout is 0.021%, the highest of any cohort.
Autoship Active (w/ items): lowest promo engagement
In the 8-14 day window: Active (w/ items) $0.34 RPD — lowest of the three groups; Active (no items) $0.68; NOT Active $1.51. Overall program RPD tells a different story: Active (no items) $0.086 → Active (w/ items) $0.076 → NOT Active $0.067. Active (w/ items) members outperform NOT Active at the all-segments level because their sends are concentrated in productive windows: only ~14% land in 121+ (1.19M/month) or No Orders (0.07M/month). NOT Active members are far more lapsed: ~74% of their sends land in 121+ (7.79M/month) or No Orders (5.26M/month) where per-send returns approach zero, dragging their overall RPD to $0.067. However, when No Orders is excluded, NOT Active recovers to $0.090 — above Active (w/ items) at $0.076 — confirming that among members who have ordered, NOT Active members are more valuable per send. Autoship cron orders do not appear in email attribution data, so these differences reflect genuine engagement patterns. A holdout will confirm how much incremental lift promo sends deliver on top of existing autoship cadence. Cadence and reactivation recommendations for each group are covered in items 4 and 5 of Recommended Next Steps.
NOT Active: largest suppression opportunity
Autoship NOT Active members account for 7.8M avg monthly 121+ sends and 5.3M No Orders sends. RPD is $0.004 and $0.001 respectively, with optout rates of 0.015–0.018%. These are non-autoship members with no subscription activity, making them the clearest suppression candidates.

Recommended Next Steps

Holdout is prerequisite — run before acting on items 2–5

The following steps are prioritized based on this analysis. Item 1 (holdout) is the prerequisite for items 3, 4, 6, and 7. Items 2 and 5 can run independently — item 2 while the holdout is in flight, item 5 once the engagement approach for that group is defined. Run the holdout for a minimum of 4–6 weeks before actioning the holdout-dependent items.

1
Run a holdout on 121+, No Orders, and 15–30 days as a reference baseline
The holdout answers one question: how much of the revenue attributed to 121+ and No Orders sends is incremental vs. organic? A holdout withholds a portion of each segment from sends and compares organic purchase rates against the mailed group. Primary cells: 121+ and No Orders, stratified by autoship status — run NOT Active separately from Active (w/ items), since their suppression cases are different. Include 15–30 as a reference baseline to see where email incrementality begins to drop off, which will inform where any future suppression threshold should sit. Do not hold out 4–7 or 8–14 day members — these segments have no planned changes and withholding emails from the highest-value window carries unnecessary revenue risk. No logic changes required.
High
2
Route No Orders members (no click in 180+ days) out of the promo stream into a re-engagement flow
Daily promos assume purchase intent that No Orders members haven't demonstrated. A re-engagement flow can lead with a stronger offer suited to first-time buyers. Measure first-purchase CVR vs. the promo control group and optout rate delta. Members who don't re-engage after the sequence move to 1×/week cadence. Expected list health benefit: ~1,400 fewer optouts/month from removing this cohort from daily sends. Run after holdout establishes the baseline.
High
3
Reduce 121+ cadence from daily to 3×/week — start with non-autoship, non-Power Members
Reducing cadence for the lowest-return lapsed segment cuts send volume, optout accumulation, and deliverability risk. Start with non-autoship non-Power Members — this group has the weakest engagement and no autoship cadence to complicate the measurement. Expected list health benefit: ~800–1,000 fewer optouts/month for this cohort at 3×/week vs. daily. Size the revenue impact against holdout data before setting the final reduction target.
High
4
Differentiate cadence for Active (w/ items) members in lapsed windows — after holdout
Active (w/ items) members already purchase consistently via autoship. If holdout confirms low incrementality for this group, reduce to 3×/week at 61-90 and 121+ days (where they return $0.009 and $0.003 RPD respectively) while maintaining daily sends in the 4–30 day window. Their lapsed exposure is smaller than NOT Active — only 14% of their sends are in 121+ or No Orders — so the revenue risk of cadence reduction here is limited. Run after holdout results confirm the incrementality baseline for this group.
Medium
5
Investigate whether a dedicated flow can drive autoship adoption for Active (no items) members
Active (no items) members are the most responsive autoship group overall ($0.086 RPD) and represent a warm audience — they have accounts but no active subscriptions. The email engagement data supports a targeted effort to prompt subscription adoption, but the conversion path (getting members to add subscribed items) is not a straightforward CRM-only action and likely requires product or lifecycle coordination. Worth investigating what lever drives subscription adoption for this group before building a dedicated flow. Maintain current promo cadence in the meantime; do not suppress.
Medium
6
Tighten No Orders engagement suppression threshold from 30 to 15–20 days inactivity
The current 30-day threshold may route members with recent activity to reduced cadence too early. Tightening to 15–20 days expands the full-cadence audience in the 4–30 day window. Validate against optout rate and CVR before broad rollout.
Medium
7
Add secondary engagement threshold for 121+: no click in 60–90 days → further cadence reduction
121+ members with no click in 60–90 days show near-zero incremental purchase probability. A secondary suppression layer reduces send volume and optout risk with minimal revenue impact. Run after the primary cadence reduction test.Holdout results will inform the expected revenue trade-off before this threshold is applied.
